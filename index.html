Date: Dec 2024

Model Selection in Linear Regression: ANSUR II Anthropometric Dataset (FR)

This project focuses on selecting the most relevant variables to predict body weight from the ANSUR II dataset, which includes 93 anthropometric measurements from over 6,000 individuals. Using stepwise selection methods (Backward and Forward), the study evaluates models based on multiple criteria, such as Adjusted R², AIC, BIC, and PRESS. Dimensionality reduction techniques, including PCA and clustering, are applied to streamline the variable selection process while maintaining high predictive accuracy. The results highlight key predictors of body weight, offering insights into modeling strategies for complex datasets.

Date: Apr 2024

Study of the Concept of Average in a Complex Geographical Region: Croatia (FR)

This project explores the calculation and definition of an average in a non-convex geographical context, specifically focusing on Croatia. Given Croatia's crescent-shaped, non-convex structure, conventional averaging methods may result in a mean value that lies outside the country's borders—an impractical and unacceptable outcome. The research seeks to develop an alternative definition of "average" that remains within the geographic boundaries, maintaining the properties of a traditional average when applied to convex spaces.
